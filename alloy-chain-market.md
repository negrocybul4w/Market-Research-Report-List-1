<p><p>Alloy chains are categorized by their grades, which indicate their strength and suitability for various applications. G100 chains are high-strength and suitable for lifting heavy loads, making them ideal for industrial settings. G80 chains are widely used in rigging and lifting as they balance strength and weight. G70 chains are often employed in transportation, particularly for heavy-duty towing. G43 chains are lighter, offering flexibility for less demanding tasks. G30 chains are the lowest grade, typically used in non-critical applications.</p></p>
