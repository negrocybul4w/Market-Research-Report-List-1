<p><p>Biophysical Environmental Assessments consist of two primary market types: terrestrial and aquatic assessments. Terrestrial assessments evaluate land-based ecosystems, focusing on flora, fauna, soil quality, and habitats to assess impacts from human activities or natural changes. In contrast, aquatic assessments examine water bodies, analyzing parameters like water quality, biodiversity, and ecological health. Both market types are crucial for environmental management, providing data that aids in regulatory compliance, conservation efforts, and sustainable development practices across various industries.</p></p>
