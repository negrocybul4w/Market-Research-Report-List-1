<p><p>The glucose market is primarily divided into two types: monohydrate glucose and anhydrous glucose. Monohydrate glucose contains water molecules in its structure, making it suitable for applications where moisture retention is beneficial, such as in food products and pharmaceuticals. In contrast, anhydrous glucose is free from water, offering higher purity and stability, which is preferred in industrial applications like fermentation and biochemical processes. Both types cater to diverse sectors, including food, beverages, and health products.</p></p>
