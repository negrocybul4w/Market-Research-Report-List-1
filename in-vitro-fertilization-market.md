<p><p>In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is a medical procedure that involves the fertilization of an egg with sperm outside the body, in a laboratory setting. The process typically includes stimulating the ovaries to produce multiple eggs, retrieving these eggs, and then combining them with sperm to create embryos. Once viable embryos are formed, one or more are selected for transfer into the uterus, with the goal of achieving a successful pregnancy.</p><p>The In Vitro Fertilization Market is experiencing robust growth, driven by increasing infertility rates, advancing reproductive technologies, and heightened awareness of assisted reproductive techniques. Enhanced success rates of IVF procedures due to technological innovations, such as preimplantation genetic testing and improved embryo culture methods, are further propelling market demand. Moreover, the growing acceptance of IVF among diverse demographics, along with expanding insurance coverage, is contributing to market expansion.</p><p>The IVF Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.3% during the forecast period. <p><p>The in vitro fertilization (IVF) market is categorized into four main types: Fresh Non-Donor, Frozen Non-Donor, Fresh Donor, and Frozen Donor. Fresh Non-Donor involves the use of a woman's own eggs and sperm collected simultaneously. Frozen Non-Donor utilizes previously frozen eggs or embryos from the same couple. Fresh Donor refers to using eggs from a donor, while Frozen Donor employs eggs or embryos from a donor that have been cryopreserved. Each type addresses different patient needs and preferences in reproductive assistance.</p></p>
