<p><p>One-dimensional diagnostic ultrasound devices are categorized into various types based on their imaging capabilities and applications. A-Type (Amplitude mode) ultrasound provides a single-dimensional view, primarily used for measuring distances within a medium. M-Type (Motion mode) offers real-time motion visualizations, commonly utilized in cardiology to assess heart function. D-Type (Doppler mode) specializes in blood flow analysis, detecting speed and direction of blood vessels. Other devices may combine features or serve specialized clinical purposes, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and versatility.</p></p>
